### What is DigiS?
DigiS is the Center for Digital Society at Fondazione Bruno Kessler (FBK). It brings together computer science, social sciences and the humanities to study how digital technologies shape society and how they can be designed and governed responsibly.

### How does DigiS work with public institutions?
DigiS collaborates with cities, regions and national institutions to support evidence-based policymaking, digital transformation and the development of digital public infrastructures such as data spaces and civic digital twins.

### What research areas does DigiS cover?
DigiS spans responsible AI, digital ethics and governance, computational social science, digital humanities, language and media technologies, data literacy, edge AI, mobility and environmental monitoring.

### Can organisations collaborate with DigiS?
Yes. DigiS works with public administrations, schools, cultural organisations, companies and international partners on research projects, training programmes and co-designed digital solutions.

### Does DigiS produce open-source tools?
Many DigiS projects generate open-source software, datasets and methodologies aligned with the principles of digital commons and FAIR data practices.

### How can I stay informed about DigiS activities?
You can follow updates through FBK channels, project websites, publications and events. For specific enquiries, use the contact form or write to the DigiS email address.
